Books I use and Recommend

[PCI Architecture|USB|Cryptography]

Books on PCI Architecture

  1. PCI System Architecture(4th Edition) by Inc. MindShare
    A Must Needed Reference for PCI Architecture
  2. PCI-X System Architecture by Inc. MindShare, Tom Shanley
    Similar reference for PCI-X Architecture
  3. PCI Bus Demystified by Doug Abbott
    Needed for a good understanding of PCI Architecture

Books on USB

  1. USB Complete: Everything You Need to Develop Custom USB Peripherals by Jan Axelson
    A great Book to get to know USB. Clearly describes the internals of USB
  2. USB Design by Example: A Practical Guide to Building I/O Devices by John Hyde
    Excellent for USB newbie. Helps quickly understand
  3. Universal Serial Bus System Architecture by Inc. MindShare, Don Anderson
    In depth explanation of USB specification

Cryptography Books

  1. Cryptography by Nigel Smart (Paperback)
    simple and exquisite in its detail but glosses over symmetric key cryptosystems

  2. RSA Security's Official Guide to Cryptography by Steve Burnett, Stephen Paine
  3. Practical Cryptography by Niels Ferguson (Author), Bruce Schneier (Author)
  4. Modern Cryptography by Wenbo Mao (Author)
  5. Cryptography by Douglas Stinson
  6. Applied Cryptography by Bruce Schneier (Author)
  7. Handbook of Applied Cryptography by Alfred J. Menezes, et al (Hardcover)
    A superb handbook for beginners and practitioners. Technical but slightly outdated
  8. Practical Cryptography by Niels Ferguson, Bruce Schneier
  9. Applied Cryptography: Protocols, Algorithms, and Source Code in C, Second Edition
    by Bruce Schneier
  10. RSA Security's Official Guide to Cryptography by Steve Burnett, Stephen Paine (Paperback)
    This book is written by somebody who has actually implemented what he writes about. An excellent overview but lacking technical bite.
  11. Cryptography: Theory and Practice (Discrete Mathematics and Its Applications) by Douglas R. Stinson (Hardcover - March 1995)
    This is an eclectic mix of cryptographic concepts; a bit dated, but lots of good (simple) mathematics A must for serious beginners.
  12. The Codebreakers : The Comprehensive History of Secret Communication from Ancient Times to the Internet  by David Kahn
