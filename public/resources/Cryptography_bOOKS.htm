Cryptography Books

 

  1. Cryptography by Nigel Smart (Paperback)

This textbook for budding cryptographers is simple and exquisite in its detail but glosses over symmetric key cryptosystems

 

  1. RSA Security's Official Guide to Cryptography by Steve Burnett, Stephen Paine

 

  1. Practical Cryptography by Niels Ferguson (Author), Bruce Schneier (Author)

 

  1. Modern Cryptography by Wenbo Mao (Author)

 

  1. Cryptography by Douglas Stinson

 

  1. Applied Cryptography by Bruce Schneier (Author)

 

  1. Handbook of Applied Cryptography by Alfred J. Menezes, et al (Hardcover)
    A superb handbook for beginners and practitioners. Technical but slightly outdated

 

  1. Practical Cryptography by Niels Ferguson, Bruce Schneier

The title of this book belies its nature - a high level look at cryptography punctuated by the authors' (sometimes unconventional) opinions. But it is not explicit in providing help to implementers

 

  1. Applied Cryptography: Protocols, Algorithms, and Source Code in C, Second Edition
    by Bruce Schneier

For many, this is definitive; it's a cryptography book for the computer scientist. It's not deep, it's very dated, but it covers a lot of material in common usage.

 

  1. RSA Security's Official Guide to Cryptography by Steve Burnett, Stephen Paine (Paperback)
    This book is written by somebody who has actually implemented what he writes about. An excellent overview but lacking technical bite.

 

  1. Cryptography: Theory and Practice (Discrete Mathematics and Its Applications) by Douglas R. Stinson (Hardcover - March 1995)
    This is an eclectic mix of cryptographic concepts; a bit dated, but lots of good (simple) mathematics A must for serious beginners.

  2. The Codebreakers : The Comprehensive History of Secret Communication from Ancient Times to the Internet  by David Kahn
